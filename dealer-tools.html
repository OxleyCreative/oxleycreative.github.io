B2B automotive commerce

Tools that support the sale - and everything behind it

I helped design and build a connected platform for dealership teams to present products, build quotes, manage inventory, and keep tire stock moving.

From showroom conversation to stockroom decision

A dealership employee may start by helping a customer imagine a new set of wheels, then need to confirm availability, create a quote, and make sure the right products stay in stock. Each task is part of one experience, even when the work crosses multiple systems.

My work focused on turning those complex, data-heavy jobs into clear interfaces that help sales and inventory teams act with confidence.

Audience
Dealership sales and inventory teams
My role
Product design, UI/UX, front-end development
Domain
Automotive retail and inventory operations

The platform

Four jobs. One connected experience.

01

Show the product

Visual selling tools help staff move beyond part numbers and show customers how wheels and related products fit their vehicle and taste.

02

Create the quote

Selections become a clear, customer-ready quote without losing the product, vehicle, pricing, and availability context gathered along the way.

03

Know what is in stock

Inventory views make tire stock easier to understand, helping teams spot what is available, what is moving, and what needs attention.

04

Replenish intelligently

Auto-replenishment reduces repetitive ordering work and supports healthier stock levels while keeping people in control of exceptions.

Design challenge

Make powerful tools feel ready for the counter

Business software can expose every rule, field, and edge case at once. Dealership tools have an added constraint: they often support a live customer conversation, so the interface needs to be fast, credible, and easy to explain while still handling operational detail.

ExploreVehicle + product
RecommendFit + preference
QuotePrice + availability
FulfillStock + replenishment

Design principles

Clarity across the entire workflow

Keep context visible

Vehicle, product, customer, and availability information should travel with the user instead of being re-entered at every step.

Reveal complexity progressively

Lead with the decisions people make most often, then surface detailed controls and exceptions when they become relevant.

Use a shared visual language

Consistent patterns across selling and inventory tools reduce relearning and make the platform feel like one dependable system.

Design for real work

Readable hierarchy, direct language, and strong feedback matter when someone is moving quickly or working with a customer beside them.

What this work demonstrates

The platform brought together customer-facing presentation and behind-the-scenes operations. That breadth required more than isolated screens: it required understanding how dealership teams think, how product and inventory data connect, and where a design system can create consistency across very different jobs.

It is the kind of work I enjoy most - learning a complicated domain, collaborating across product and development, and making the resulting experience feel straightforward to the person using it.
